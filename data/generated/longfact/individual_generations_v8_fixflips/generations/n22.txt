The Karolinska Institute, located in Stockholm, Sweden, is one of the world's leading medical universities, founded in 1810. It is renowned for its research and education in health sciences, particularly in fields such as medicine, public health, and biomedical research. The institute is also famous for awarding the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine, with notable laureates including Albert A. Michelson in 1907 and Tu Youyou in 2015. As of 2023, it has approximately 6,000 students and 4,000 employees, contributing to its status as a prominent center for medical research and education. The institute's campus is situated in the Solna municipality, and it collaborates closely with the Karolinska University Hospital, enhancing its clinical research capabilities.
