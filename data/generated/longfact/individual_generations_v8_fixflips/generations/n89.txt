St. Peter’s Basilica, located in Vatican City, is one of the largest and most renowned churches in the world, with a length of 218 meters (720 feet) and a dome that rises 136.57 meters (448.1 feet) high, designed by Michelangelo. Construction began in 1506 under Pope Julius II and was completed in 1626 during the papacy of Pope Urban VIII, involving contributions from notable architects such as Bramante, Michelangelo, and Bernini. The basilica is built over the traditional burial site of Saint Peter, one of Jesus' apostles and the first Pope, making it a significant pilgrimage site for Catholics. The interior features remarkable artworks, including Michelangelo's Pietà and Bernini's Baldachin, and it can accommodate around 20,000 worshippers. St. Peter’s Basilica is also the site of many important events, including papal conclaves and the canonization of saints, with the most recent canonization ceremony taking place on May 15, 2022.
