Cloud9 is a prominent eSports organization based in the United States, founded in 2013 by Jack and Paullie, with its headquarters in Los Angeles, California. The organization has fielded teams in various games, including League of Legends, Counter-Strike: Global Offensive, and Valorant, achieving significant success; for instance, their League of Legends team won the North American League of Legends Championship Series (NALCS) in 2013 and 2021. Notable players associated with Cloud9 include Tyler "Tyler1" Steinkamp, who gained fame through his League of Legends streams, and TenZ, a standout player in Valorant who was loaned to Cloud9 from Cloud9's Valorant team before later signing with Sentinels. Cloud9 has also participated in major tournaments, such as the League of Legends World Championship, where they reached the quarterfinals in 2018. The organization is known for its distinctive blue and white branding and has a strong fan base, often engaging with its community through social media and live events.
