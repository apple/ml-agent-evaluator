Marillyn Hewson served as the CEO of Lockheed Martin from January 2013 until June 2020, becoming the first woman to lead the company. Under her leadership, Lockheed Martin's revenue grew significantly, reaching approximately $65 billion in 2019, driven by key programs such as the F-35 Lightning II fighter jet and the Orion spacecraft. Hewson emphasized innovation and technology, overseeing the acquisition of Aerojet Rocketdyne in 2020, which aimed to enhance the company's capabilities in propulsion systems. She also focused on workforce development, launching initiatives to increase diversity and inclusion within the company, which included partnerships with organizations like the National Society of Black Engineers. Hewson's tenure was marked by a strong commitment to corporate responsibility, as she led efforts to improve sustainability and reduce the company's carbon footprint.
