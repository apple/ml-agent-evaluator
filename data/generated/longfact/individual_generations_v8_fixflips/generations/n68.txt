Kevin Mitnick is a renowned cybersecurity analyst and former hacker, often referred to as one of the most famous computer criminals in history. Born on August 6, 1963, in Los Angeles, California, he gained notoriety in the 1980s and 1990s for his high-profile hacks, including breaching the networks of major corporations like Nokia, IBM, and Motorola. In 1995, he was arrested by the FBI after a lengthy pursuit, which included a dramatic chase involving the FBI and the U.S. Secret Service, and he ultimately served five years in prison. After his release in 2000, Mitnick transitioned to a career in cybersecurity consulting and became a public speaker, authoring books such as "The Art of Deception" and "The Art of Intrusion." Today, he is the Chief Hacking Officer at Mitnick Security Consulting, LLC, where he advises organizations on security practices and vulnerabilities.
