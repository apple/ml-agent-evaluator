"Joker," directed by Todd Phillips and released on October 4, 2019, is a psychological thriller that explores the origin of the iconic DC Comics character, the Joker. Joaquin Phoenix stars as Arthur Fleck, a struggling stand-up comedian with a mental illness who becomes increasingly alienated from society in Gotham City during the early 1980s. The film delves into themes of mental health, societal neglect, and the impact of trauma, culminating in Arthur's transformation into the infamous criminal mastermind. Notably, the film features a haunting score by Hildur Guðnadóttir, which won an Academy Award for Best Original Score. "Joker" received critical acclaim, earning Phoenix the Academy Award for Best Actor for his chilling portrayal of the character.
