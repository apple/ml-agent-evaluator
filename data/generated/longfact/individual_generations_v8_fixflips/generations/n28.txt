Charlize Theron is a South African-American actress and producer, born on August 7, 1975, in Benoni, South Africa. She gained international fame for her role in the 2003 film "Monster," for which she won the Academy Award for Best Actress, portraying serial killer Aileen Wuornos. Theron has starred in numerous successful films, including "Mad Max: Fury Road" (2015), which earned her a BAFTA nomination, and "Atomic Blonde" (2017), showcasing her action-hero capabilities. In addition to her acting career, she founded the Charlize Theron Africa Outreach Project in 2007 to support HIV/AIDS prevention efforts in South Africa. Theron has also received several accolades, including a Golden Globe Award for her performance in "North Country" (2005) and a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame, awarded in 2007.
