The Blue Note is a renowned jazz club located in Greenwich Village, New York City, established in 1981. It has hosted numerous legendary jazz musicians, including icons like Miles Davis, John Coltrane, and Herbie Hancock, and continues to feature contemporary artists such as Robert Glasper and Norah Jones. The club has a capacity of about 200 people, providing an intimate setting for live performances, which typically occur twice nightly at 8:00 PM and 10:30 PM. In addition to its New York location, the Blue Note brand has expanded internationally, with clubs in cities like Tokyo and Milan. The venue is also known for its commitment to jazz education, hosting events like the "Blue Note Jazz Festival," which showcases a diverse lineup of artists each summer.
