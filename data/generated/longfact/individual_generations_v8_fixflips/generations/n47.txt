Larry Page is an American computer scientist and entrepreneur best known as one of the co-founders of Google, which he established alongside Sergey Brin in September 1998 while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University. Page served as the CEO of Google from its founding until 2001, and then again from 2011 to 2015, when he became the CEO of Alphabet Inc., Google's parent company, after a corporate restructuring. Under his leadership, Google introduced several key products, including Google Search, Google AdWords, and Android, which significantly shaped the tech landscape. Page was born on March 26, 1973, in East Lansing, Michigan, and he holds a Bachelor’s degree in Computer Engineering from the University of Michigan and a Master’s degree from Stanford University. As of 2021, Page's net worth was estimated to be over $100 billion, making him one of the wealthiest individuals in the world.
