The University of Texas MD Anderson Cancer Center, located in Houston, Texas, is one of the world's leading cancer research and treatment institutions, founded in 1941. It is part of the University of Texas System and is recognized for its comprehensive cancer care, ranking consistently among the top cancer hospitals in the United States according to U.S. News & World Report. MD Anderson is home to over 21,000 employees, including more than 1,000 faculty members, and it conducts extensive research, with an annual budget exceeding $1 billion dedicated to cancer research. The center is known for pioneering treatments and therapies, such as immunotherapy, and has been instrumental in developing groundbreaking clinical trials, including those led by notable researchers like Dr. James Allison, who won the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 2018 for his work in cancer immunotherapy. Additionally, MD Anderson hosts events like the annual "Moonshot" initiative, launched in 2012, aimed at accelerating cancer research and reducing cancer deaths by 50% over the next 25 years.
