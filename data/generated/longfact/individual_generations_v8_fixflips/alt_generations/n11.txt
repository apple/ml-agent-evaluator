Rockstar Games is a prominent video game developer and publisher known for creating some of the most successful and critically acclaimed video game franchises in the industry. Founded in December 1998, it is a subsidiary of Take-Two Interactive and has its headquarters in New York City. The company is best known for the "Grand Theft Auto" series, with "Grand Theft Auto V" (released in September 2013) becoming one of the best-selling video games of all time, selling over 185 million copies worldwide. Another significant franchise is "Red Dead Redemption," with "Red Dead Redemption 2" launching in October 2018 and receiving widespread acclaim for its storytelling and open-world design. Key figures in the company include Sam Houser, one of the co-founders, and Dan Houser, who played a crucial role in writing and producing many of the games. Rockstar Games is also known for its controversial themes and immersive gameplay, often set in fictionalized versions of real-world locations, such as Los Santos (based on Los Angeles) and Liberty City (based on New York City). The company has received numerous awards, including multiple BAFTA Game Awards and Game Developers Choice Awards. Rockstar's commitment to high-quality production values and engaging narratives has solidified its reputation as a leader in the gaming industry.
