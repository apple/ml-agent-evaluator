Charlize Theron is a South African-American actress and producer, born on August 7, 1975, in Benoni, South Africa. She gained international fame for her role in the 2003 film "Monster," for which she won the Academy Award for Best Actress, portraying serial killer Aileen Wuornos. Theron has starred in numerous successful films, including "Mad Max: Fury Road" (2015), which earned her a BAFTA nomination, and "Atomic Blonde" (2017), showcasing her action star capabilities. In addition to her acting career, she founded the Charlize Theron Africa Outreach Project in 2007, aimed at combating HIV/AIDS among African youth. Theron has also been recognized for her work in producing, with her company, Denver and Delilah Productions, producing films like "Tully" (2018) and "The Old Guard" (2020).
