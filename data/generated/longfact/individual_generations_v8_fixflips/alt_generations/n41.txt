Serena Williams is a professional tennis player from the United States, widely regarded as one of the greatest athletes in the history of the sport. Born on September 26, 1981, in Saginaw, Michigan, she began playing tennis at a young age, coached by her father, Richard Williams, alongside her sister Venus Williams. Over her illustrious career, Serena has won 23 Grand Slam singles titles, the most by any player in the Open Era, with her last major victory at the 2017 Australian Open. She has also achieved a career-high singles ranking of World No. 1, holding that position for a total of 319 weeks. Off the court, Williams is known for her advocacy for gender equality and racial justice, and she has launched various business ventures, including her own clothing line, Serena.
