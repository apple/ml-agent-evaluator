Jim Hackett served as the President and CEO of Ford Motor Company from May 2017 until October 2020. He succeeded Mark Fields and was brought in to lead the company through a significant transformation, focusing on electric and autonomous vehicles. Under his leadership, Ford announced a $11 billion investment in electric vehicles and unveiled the Mustang Mach-E in November 2019, marking a pivotal shift towards electrification. Hackett also emphasized a new corporate strategy called "Ford+" to enhance the company's profitability and competitiveness in the evolving automotive landscape. He stepped down in October 2020, succeeded by Jim Farley, as Ford continued to navigate the challenges of the automotive industry.
