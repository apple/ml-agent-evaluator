"Gravity" is a 2013 science fiction thriller directed by Alfonso Cuarón, featuring Sandra Bullock as Dr. Ryan Stone, a medical engineer on her first space mission, and George Clooney as veteran astronaut Matt Kowalsky. The film is set in low Earth orbit, primarily aboard the Space Shuttle Explorer and the International Space Station, and follows the harrowing events that unfold after a catastrophic debris field from a destroyed satellite strikes their shuttle, leaving them stranded in space. The film's stunning visual effects and cinematography, crafted by Emmanuel Lubezki, earned it seven Academy Awards, including Best Director for Cuarón. The narrative unfolds in real-time, with a runtime of 91 minutes, emphasizing the isolation and desperation of the characters as they struggle to survive and find a way back to Earth. "Gravity" was released on October 4, 2013, and grossed over $723 million worldwide, making it one of the highest-grossing films of that year.
