"Joker," directed by Todd Phillips and released on October 4, 2019, is a psychological thriller that explores the origin of the iconic DC Comics character, the Joker. The film stars Joaquin Phoenix as Arthur Fleck, a struggling stand-up comedian with a mental illness who transforms into the infamous criminal after facing societal rejection and personal tragedy. Set in Gotham City during the early 1980s, the narrative delves into themes of mental health, class struggle, and the impact of societal neglect, culminating in a violent uprising led by Arthur. The film features notable performances from Robert De Niro as Murray Franklin, a talk show host who plays a pivotal role in Arthur's descent into madness. "Joker" received critical acclaim, winning the Golden Lion at the Venice Film Festival and earning Phoenix an Academy Award for Best Actor for his haunting portrayal of the character.
