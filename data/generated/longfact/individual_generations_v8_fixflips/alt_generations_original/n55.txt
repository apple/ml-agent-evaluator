Indra Nooyi is an Indian-American business executive who served as the CEO and Chairperson of PepsiCo from 2006 to 2018, making her one of the most prominent women in global business. Born on October 28, 1955, in Chennai, India, she earned a Bachelor's degree in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics from Madras Christian College and an MBA from the Indian Institute of Management Calcutta, followed by a Master's degree in Public and Private Management from Yale University. Under her leadership, PepsiCo's revenue grew from $35 billion to $63.5 billion, and she was instrumental in shifting the company's focus towards healthier products, launching initiatives like "Performance with Purpose." Nooyi has been recognized with numerous awards, including being named one of Forbes' World's 100 Most Powerful Women multiple times, and she was inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame in 2021. After stepping down as CEO, she has continued to influence the business world through her work on various boards, including Amazon and the International Cricket Council (ICC).
