The Karolinska Institute, located in Solna, Sweden, is one of the world's leading medical universities, founded in 1810. It is renowned for its research and education in medicine and health sciences, and it plays a pivotal role in the selection of Nobel Prize laureates in Physiology or Medicine. The institute has produced notable alumni, including Nobel laureates such as Paul Langerhans (1889) and Svante Pääbo (2022). With over 6,000 students and 4,000 employees, it emphasizes a strong research environment, contributing significantly to advancements in areas like cancer, cardiovascular diseases, and neuroscience. The Karolinska Institute also collaborates with various hospitals and research institutions, enhancing its impact on global health.
