The Blue Note is a renowned jazz club located in Greenwich Village, New York City, established in 1981. It has hosted numerous legendary jazz musicians, including icons like Miles Davis, John Coltrane, and Herbie Hancock, and continues to feature contemporary artists such as Robert Glasper and Norah Jones. The club has a seating capacity of about 200 and offers live performances nightly, often with two shows at 8:00 PM and 10:30 PM. In addition to its New York location, the Blue Note brand has expanded internationally, with clubs in cities like Tokyo and Milan. The venue is celebrated not only for its music but also for its intimate atmosphere and high-quality dining options, making it a staple in the jazz community.
