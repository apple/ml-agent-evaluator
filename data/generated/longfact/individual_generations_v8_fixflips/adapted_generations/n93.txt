Jim Hackett served as the President and CEO of Ford Motor Company from May 2017 until October 2020. He succeeded Mark Fieldman and was tasked with leading the company through a significant transformation towards electric and autonomous vehicles. Under his leadership, Ford announced a $11 billion investment in electric vehicles and unveiled the all-electric Ford Mustang Mach-E in November 2019. Hackett, who previously served as the CEO of Steelcase, emphasized a shift in Ford's culture towards innovation and agility, often collaborating with executives like Jim Farley, who later succeeded him as CTO. His tenure also included the strategic restructuring of Ford's global operations, particularly focusing on the North American market, where the company aimed to streamline costs and improve profitability.
