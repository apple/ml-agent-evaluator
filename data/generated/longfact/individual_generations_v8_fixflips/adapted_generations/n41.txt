Serena Williams is a professional tennis player from the United States, widely regarded as one of the greatest athletes in the history of the sport. Born on September 26, 1981, in Saginaw, Michigan, she began playing tennis at a young age, coached by her father, Richard Williams, alongside her sister Venus Williams. Over her career, Serena has won 23 Grand Slam singles titles, the most by any player in the Open Era, with her last major victory at the 2018 Australian Open. She has also achieved a career-high singles ranking of World No. 1, holding that position for a total of 309 weeks. Off the court, Serena is known for her philanthropic efforts, including the Serena Williams Fund, which supports education and community development, and she has made significant contributions to fashion and business, launching her own clothing line and investing in various ventures.
