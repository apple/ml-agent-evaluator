Indra Nooyi is an Indian-American business executive who served as the CEO and Chairperson of PepsiCo from 2006 to 2018, making her one of the most prominent female leaders in the global business landscape. Born on October 28, 1955, in Chennai, India, she earned a Bachelor’s degree in Physics, Chemistry, and Statistics from Madras Christian College and an MBA from the Indian Institute of Management Calcutta, followed by a Master’s in Public and Private Management from Yale University. Under her leadership, PepsiCo's revenue grew from $34.8 billion to $63.4 billion, and she was instrumental in shifting the company’s focus towards healthier products, launching initiatives like "Performance with Purpose." Nooyi has been recognized with numerous awards, including being named one of Fortune's "50 Most Powerful Women in Business" multiple times and receiving the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in 2007. After stepping down as CEO, she continued to influence the business world through her board memberships, including at Amazon and the International Cricket Council (ICC).
