"Gravity" is a 2013 science fiction thriller directed by Alfonso Cuarón, featuring Sandra Bullock as Dr. Ryan Stone, a medical engineer on her first space mission, and George Clooney as veteran astronaut Matt Kowalsky. The film is set in low Earth orbit, primarily around the International Space Station (ISS) and the Hubble Space Telescope, and it begins with a catastrophic event when debris from a destroyed satellite collides with their shuttle, leaving them stranded in space. The movie showcases the struggle for survival as Stone and Kowalsky attempt to navigate back to safety, facing numerous challenges including loss of communication and dwindling oxygen supplies. "Gravity" was released on October 4, 2013, and received critical acclaim for its groundbreaking visual effects, winning six Academy Awards, including Best Director for Cuarón. The film's runtime is 89 minutes, and it grossed over $723 million worldwide, making it one of the highest-grossing films of 2013.
