The Raspberry Pi Zero is a compact, low-cost single-board computer developed by the Raspberry Pi Foundation, first released in November 2015. It features a Broadcom BCM2845 processor, 512 MB of RAM, and a mini HDMI port, making it suitable for various DIY projects and embedded applications. The Pi Zero measures just 65mm x 30mm, making it significantly smaller than its predecessors, and it is often used in projects like portable gaming consoles and IoT devices. In January 2016, the Raspberry Pi Zero W Lite was introduced, adding built-in Wi-Fi and Bluetooth capabilities, further expanding its versatility. The Raspberry Pi community has embraced the Zero, with numerous tutorials and projects shared online, showcasing its use in everything from robotics to home automation.
